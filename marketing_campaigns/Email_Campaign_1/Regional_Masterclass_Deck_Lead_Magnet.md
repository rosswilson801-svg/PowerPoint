## Slide 2: The "Do Now" (Entrance Ticket)
**Objective:** To understand what anxiety feels like physically and mentally, and to identify culturally appropriate coping strategies.

**Do Now Activity:**
Think of a time this week when you felt "under pressure" (e.g., before a test, a sports match, or meeting new people).
*   Write down 1 physical feeling you had (e.g., racing heart).
*   Write down 1 thought you had (e.g., "I can't do this").
*Be ready to share (opting in is encouraged, but not forced).*

---

## Slide 3: Pace & Purpose
**What is Anxiety?**
It is a normal, natural human response to stress or danger. It is our body's "alarm system."
In many international and high-achieving school cultures, academic pressure is a leading cause of this alarm system firing when there is no physical danger.

**The Science of Stress:**
*   Cortisol & Adrenaline release.
*   "Fight, Flight, or Freeze" response.

---

## Slide 4: All Opt-In (Discussion)
**Regional Context Check:**
How is stress viewed in your culture or your family?
*   A) It's openly discussed.
*   B) It's seen as a sign of working hard.
*   C) It's something we don't talk about much.

*Teacher Note: Use No-Hands Up questioning to gently probe responses. Validate that different cultures have different approaches to discussing mental health.*

---

## Slide 5: Active Participation (The Stress Bucket)
**Activity: The Stress Bucket Model**
Imagine your capacity to handle stress is a bucket.
1.  **Water flowing in:** Exams, peer pressure, social media, changing schools/countries (Expat stress).
2.  **Water flowing out (Coping strategies):** Taking breaks, talking to friends, exercise, mindfulness.

*Task:* Draw your own stress bucket. What fills it? What are your "taps" to empty it?

---

## Slide 6: Modelling Strategies
**Culturally Responsive Coping:**
What works for one person might not work for another. Let's look at healthy "taps":

1.  **The 4-7-8 Breathing Technique:** Grounding yourself in the present.
2.  **Cognitive Reframing:** Changing "I must be perfect" to "I will do my best."
3.  **Seeking Support:** Identifying trusted adults in school (Form Tutor, Pastoral Head, Counsellor).

## Slide 7: Feedback & Reflection (Exit Ticket)
**Review of Learning:**
1.  Name one physical symptom of anxiety.
2.  List one healthy coping strategy you will try this week.
