# 🌍 Market Research: International School PSHE (2026)

## Market Size & Growth (The $67 Billion Opportunity)
The international schools market is experiencing explosive growth.
*   **Scale:** As of 2025/2026, there are over **15,000 international schools** worldwide, enrolling **7.7 million students**.
*   **Revenue:** The sector generates **$67.3 billion to $69.3 billion** in annual fee income.
*   **Growth Drivers:** An expanding expatriate population and a massive shift towards "hybrid curricula" that blend international standards (like the English National Curriculum) with localized, culturally relevant content.

## The Purchasing Cycle (When They Buy)
Understanding the school budget timeline is critical for Clarity's sales efforts:
*   **Nov - April (Information Gathering):** Schools assess needs and research suppliers. *This is when our marketing must peak.*
*   **March - June (Budget Approval):** Departmental allocations are finalized.
*   **May - August (Purchasing):** Peak buying season for curriculum resources.

## Market Dynamics & The "Transient" Problem
The international school market is defined by high transience. Students and staff move frequently, and classrooms are deeply multicultural. 

### Key Characteristics
1.  **Cultural Friction & The BSO Mandate:** The British Schools Overseas (BSO) inspection framework *explicitly requires* schools to adapt their PSHE education to the "cultural, social, and legal contexts of the host country." Using generic UK resources often fails this inspection standard.
2.  **The "Cascade" Failure:** Training is often passed down from a single PSHE lead to non-specialist form tutors. This results in inconsistent delivery, especially for sensitive topics where teachers lack confidence.
3.  **Framework Fatigue:** Schools try to balance the rigorous English National Curriculum with local statutory requirements (e.g., UAE Moral Education), leading to a confusing blend of standards.

## The Buying Committee
*   **The Champion (End User):** The Head of Pastoral Care or Head of Year. They are drowning in admin and trying to support teachers who feel uncomfortable delivering sensitive material.
*   **The Economic Buyer:** The Principal or Curriculum Director. They care about compliance (BSO/CIS), safeguarding, and a cohesive "whole school" approach that doesn't upset conservative parent bodies.
