# 🥊 Competitor Analysis: International PSHE

## The "Legacy" UK Providers (e.g., Jigsaw, PSHE Association)
*   **What they do well:** Comprehensive, trusted by UK inspectors, robust frameworks.
*   **Where they fail internationally:** Highly UK-centric scenarios (e.g., discussing the NHS, UK law, specific UK demographics). They require massive adaptation time from international school teachers.
*   **Positioning Gap:** They are built for the UK system; we are built for the global system.

## In-House Development (The "Do It Yourself" Competitor)
*   **What they do well:** It's hyper-localized to that specific school (because the teachers wrote it).
*   **Where they fail:** It's unscalable. It relies on the enthusiasm of one or two passionate teachers. When they leave, the curriculum dies. It also lacks professional design and rigorous statutory tracking.
*   **Positioning Gap:** "Stop paying your highly qualified teachers to be amateur curriculum writers."

## Mental Health Platforms (e.g., YouHQ, Komodo)
*   **What they do well:** Student check-ins, data tracking, immediate pastoral alerts.
*   **Where they fail:** They are diagnostic, not pedagogical. They tell you a student is sad, but they don't provide the 45-minute lesson plan for Thursday period 3 on coping with anxiety.
*   **Positioning Gap:** We are the teaching content; they are the diagnostic tool. We complement them.

## The Overall Gap
No one is providing a highly-designed, rigorous curriculum that is *built from the ground up* to be culturally adaptable for the transient international school market. Clarity is the premium, "done-for-you" solution.
