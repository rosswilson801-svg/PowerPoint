# 🤕 Pain Points: Heads of Pastoral & Teachers

## The "Zero Prep" Myth
*   **Pain:** Teachers are told to deliver PSHE, but they aren't PSHE specialists. They are given a generic PDF and told to "make it work for a 45-minute lesson."
*   **Result:** Sunday nights spent building PowerPoint slides from scratch. Anxiety about getting the tone right on sensitive topics.
*   **Clarity Solution:** Fully designed, click-and-play slide decks. Zero prep required.

## The Cultural Compliance Tightrope (The BSO Trap)
*   **Pain:** The British Schools Overseas (BSO) framework requires schools to teach PSHE while simultaneously *mandating* that it be adapted to the local cultural and legal context. 
*   **Result:** Teachers are caught in the middle. They self-censor or spend hours rewriting UK resources to appease both UK inspectors and local parents.
*   **Clarity Solution:** The "Regional Overlay" system. Content that is pre-filtered for specific cultural contexts while maintaining core BSO compliance.

## The "Cascade" Dilution
*   **Pain:** The Head of Pastoral writes an amazing curriculum map, but it's delivered by 30 different form tutors with varying levels of enthusiasm and skill.
*   **Result:** The student experience of PSHE depends entirely on which tutor group they are in.
*   **Clarity Solution:** Highly structured, visually engaging lessons that "carry" the teacher. The design and script do the heavy lifting, ensuring consistency across the whole year group.

## The Admin Burden
*   **Pain:** Tracking which statutory requirements are being met across which year groups is a nightmare of spreadsheets.
*   **Result:** When inspectors (e.g., BSO, CIS) arrive, there is a mad scramble to prove what was taught.
*   **Clarity Solution:** The Curriculum Planner automatically maps lessons to global standards (UK Statutory, CASEL, etc.).
